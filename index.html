Research

Calligraphy Robots

Chinese calligraphy is a unique art form with great artistic value but difficult to master. In this paper, we formulate the calligraphy writing problem as a trajectory optimization problem, and propose an improved virtual brush model for simulating the real writing process. Our approach is inspired by pseudospectral optimal control in that we parameterize the actuator trajectory for each stroke as a Chebyshev polynomial. The proposed dynamic virtual brush model plays a key role in formulating the objective function to be optimized. Our approach shows excellent performance in drawing aesthetically pleasing characters, and does so much more efficiently than previous work, opening up the possibility to achieve real-time closed-loop control.

Non-intrusive Load Monitoring (NILM)

NILM is mainly about finding the different working states of home electronics from only their total power consumption. Electtronics such as television, refrigerator generally have some non-regular consumption pattern with big randomness (like the figure shows), however, the problem could be approached with the proposed analyzation tools. In this paper, we propose a new method based on kernel density estimation(KDE) for low frequency NILM (Non-intrusive load monitoring). The power consumption features of different kinds of electronic application are estimated from kernel density estimation. After it, we establish a simulated dataset which stores the power consumption signal of different combinations of electronic application. Now, given the total power consumption data over a period, we divide it into different stages by detecting step changes, and use a brute-force searching to match each divided stage with the simulation data to find the corresponding power consumption pattern. The proposed approach was tested with data from the GREEND public dataset, it showed good performance (93% accuracy) in the tested data.

Publication:

[1] Wang Sen, Yang Dongsheng, Guo Chuchen, "Du Shengxian, Non-intrusive Load Disaggregation Based on Kernel Density Estimation" Asia Conference on Power and Electrical Engineering 2017 [PDF]

Action Recognition

In this project, we propose a fast algorithm to achieve both action recognition and localization in videos. There are mainly three steps: human detection, frame connection, and single-person action recognition.

The key observation is that the expected output of normal human detection algorithms is bounding boxes which have continuous image coordinates in adjacent frames. As such, we propose to directly use bounding boxes as the output of human detection step but only do the calculation for a small portion of frames and let interpolation get the detection results for the other frames. The detected bounding boxes in different frames then constitute sequence with only one human, and so the action could be detected with a simpler network for higher calculation efficiency. After the speeding up in the first and third step, our algorithm calculates at a much faster speed compared with several baseline algorithms with good detection accuracy.

Report and poster:

Sen Wang, Ziyi Zhou, "Multi-Person Action Recognition Based on Deep Learning", 2018 [PDF]
